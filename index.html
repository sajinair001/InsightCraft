FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about engagement models, AI and delivery.

When should an organization engage AVIGNITY?

Organizations typically engage us when transformation initiatives are stalled or under-delivering, IT or cybersecurity risk is rising faster than maturity, leadership bandwidth is stretched, AI initiatives are generating noise instead of measurable outcomes, regulatory or audit pressure is increasing, or major change is underway (M&A, divestiture, operating model shifts). If execution risk or decision fatigue is slowing the business, it’s usually the right time to talk.

What types of organizations do you work with?

We primarily work with small to mid-size enterprises - especially industrial, manufacturing, engineering and regulated organizations. Our work is most valuable in complex, asset-heavy, or high-risk environments, though we occasionally support growth-stage organizations facing enterprise-scale challenges.

How do engagements typically start?

Most engagements begin with a focused discovery or executive briefing to understand your context, constraints and priorities, clarify the true problem and identify the biggest decision, execution and risk gaps. From there, we propose a clear, time-bound engagement aligned to outcomes.

Do you provide advisory services or hands-on execution?

Both - by design. We operate across executive advisory, hands-on delivery and program leadership and fractional CIO/CISO leadership. We stay engaged through execution, not just recommendations.

What does a typical engagement look like?

While every engagement is tailored, most follow a consistent structure: Discover (establish baseline and priorities), Design (roadmap and measures), Implement (cadence and delivery) and Optimize (refine and scale). We emphasize momentum, transparency and decision clarity throughout.

How long do engagements last?

Engagements vary based on need, from short advisory or assessments (weeks), to time-boxed transformation initiatives (months), to fractional leadership roles (ongoing, part-time). Our goal is to be effective, not embedded indefinitely.

How do you approach AI and automation?

We treat AI as a practical enabler - not a strategy on its own. AI should support human decision-making, be applied selectively to well-understood processes and deliver measurable business value. Process clarity comes before automation.

Are you vendor-agnostic?

Yes. We do not sell tools, platforms, or vendor software. Our recommendations are driven by your business context, risk profile and operational maturity, so decisions are made in your best interest.

How do you ensure AI and automation are implemented responsibly?

We design AI and automation with governance, security and accountability built in - clear ownership, transparency in decision logic and alignment to regulatory and ethical expectations. Responsible, explainable systems are essential in enterprise environments.

Do you support cybersecurity and compliance initiatives?

Yes. We support cybersecurity strategy and operating models, regulatory and audit readiness, risk governance and executive reporting and secure-by-design transformation. Our approach balances risk reduction with business enablement.

How do you measure success?

We measure success by outcomes, not activity. Common indicators include improved decision speed and clarity, reduced operational or security risk, better execution predictability, clear ownership and accountability and sustainable operating cadence. We define metrics upfront and revisit them regularly.

Do you work within constrained budgets?

Yes. We work with clients operating on limited budgets and lean teams. We prioritize focus, leverage existing investments and design engagements to deliver value without unnecessary overhead.

What is the first step if we’re interested?

The first step is a 30-minute executive conversation to understand your context, explore fit on both sides and decide whether a deeper engagement makes sense. No sales pressure, no obligation.
